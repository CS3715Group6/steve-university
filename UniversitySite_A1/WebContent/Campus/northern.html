Campuses

Northern Campus

The Steve University Northern Campus (SUNC) is a four-year undergraduate, graduate, and post-graduate campus of the Steve University. It is located in downtown Steve, Newfoundland & Labrador. Students can choose majors in business, education, nursing, computer science, information technology, criminal justice, social work, environmental science, urban studies and interdisciplinary arts and sciences.
Visit the Northern Campus.



Southern Campus

The Steve University Southern Campus (SUSC) is a four-year undergraduate and graduate campus in Steve, one of the three campuses of the Steve University. SUSC is the largest branch campus in Canada and the fastest growing four-year university in Newfoundland & Labrador. In 2014 and 2015, Steve Magazine ranked SUSC as the best university in Newfoundland & Labrador in terms of value and quality.
Visit the Southern Campus.



Eastern Campus

The Steve University Eastern Campus (SUEC), located in the suburb of Steve, began in the 1970s. Many academic programs, including the entirety of the undergraduate College of Arts and Sciences, the Steve University Law School, the School of Engineering and Applied Sciences, the School of Management, the Graduate School of Social Work, and the Graduate School of Education, as well as Lockwood Memorial Library, Capen Library, and many administrative offices, are located on SUEC.
Visit the Eastern Campus.

About Steve University Northern Campus

SUNC is an urban-serving university providing access to students in a way that transforms families and communities. We impact and inform economic development through community-engaged students and faculty. We conduct research that is of direct use to our community and region. And, most importantly, we seek to be connected to our community’s needs and aspirations.

Set in the historic Steve Station District, SUNC owes its charm to century-old, brick buildings that were built to last by businesses that depended on the railroad in the late 1880s and early 1900s. The university has earned architectural awards for transforming these buildings into modern classrooms. In the design of its beautiful campus, SUNC honors the traditions of the Northern Pacific Railroad and its part in establishing Steve as the City of Destiny.

The 46-acre campus footprint is located on a hillside overlooking the Port of Steve and Mount Unicorn, on the southern edge of downtown Steve, next to museums and the beautifully reconstructed Steve Station. Within walking distance are an array of restaurants, attractions, businesses, shops, parks, museums and historic architecture.

SUNC is accredited as a unit of the Steve University by the Northwest Commission on Colleges and Universities.

Students

SUNC’s diverse student body includes a broad range of ethnic and family backgrounds, ages, interests and experience. More than 80 percent of students transfer to SUNC from one of the many community colleges in the area or from other universities. Freshmen from high schools all over the Puget Sound and beyond make up a growing portion of students. Add to the mix students who are returning to school after years away, military personnel and their families, and professionals working on new career goals. Our students create a kaleidoscope of perspectives crucial to learning.

Learning

At SUNC, students learn in small classes, getting to know their faculty while participating in research and service projects. First-year students complete an innovative core curriculum, which blends different fields of study into interdisciplinary classes. Courses might mix art and ecology, or business and social science. The result is a rich learning environment where students are encouraged to challenge themselves.

Community

SUNC is an urban-serving university, closely linked with the Steve University Eastern and Soutern Campuses. We’re a member of the Coalition of Atlantic Research Universities, a network of public, urban, research universities committed to creating an educated workforce, building strong communities and improving the health of diverse populations.
